**Revised edition: inbound-first, cold outreach optional.**

## Bottom line up front

At €60/hr and a €0–100/month tooling budget, your fastest path to a stable pipeline is a three-channel stack — **Malt (EU freelance platform) + LinkedIn founder-led content + warm network reactivation** — anchored by a single sharp niche (*"AI workflow automation and custom web apps for teams on n8n + Next.js + LLMs"*).

Expect **first paid client in 6–12 weeks**, 3–5 recurring clients by month 6. This timeline is slower than the high-volume cold outreach path, but:

- Zero tool costs vs €200+/month
- Higher-quality clients (they sought you out, not the other way around)
- Aligned with Czech/Slovak cultural norms (warm referrals > cold email)
- Sustainable — you'll actually execute it rather than resent it

**Keep Eviro as a public case study and credibility asset, not a parallel pursuit.** It's your single most valuable piece of marketing content, but productising it must wait until consulting cash flow is stable (~€8–10k MRR).

The biggest risk in your situation isn't channel selection — it's **fragmentation across too many services, geographies, and platforms at €50–60/hour**. What follows is a concrete 12-week execution sequence after positioning, pricing, and channel decisions.

## Positioning: pick one sharp beachhead, not three services

A "AI automation + web dev + SaaS" generalist positioning is the single most expensive mistake you can make in 2026. The core thesis — echoed by Philip Morgan, Jonathan Stark, and Brennan Dunn — is that **generalists lose to specialists on visibility, trust, and rate**. At €50–60/hour you're competing with an oversupplied senior React/Next.js market where Western European generalist freelance rates have been flat year-over-year, and Eastern European rates have stopped converging upward. The only segment growing 18–25% per year in rate is **AI/ML specialists** — that's the door you must walk through.

### Recommended beachhead

> *"AI workflow automation and internal tools for EU SME/mid-market SaaS and e-commerce companies, built on n8n + Next.js + LLMs."*

This positioning compounds four market signals:

1. **Malt reported a 230% year-over-year rise in AI-related project volume** in 2025
2. **n8n workflow implementations** are selling at €3.5k–€10k setup + €1k–€2.5k/month retainers
3. **n8n is 10–20× cheaper than Zapier** for clients — you have a margin story
4. **Eviro is a living case study** in exactly this category (automating review workflows for local businesses = AI automation for SMBs)

### Frank's override (April 2026)

The site's current hero tagline drops "across Europe" in favour of the broader **"AI automation and custom web apps for ambitious teams"** — by explicit choice, to avoid geo-locked positioning and keep audience broad. EU context stays factual in the About copy, not as a positioning constraint.

### What to drop from primary positioning

- **Web3 / Solidity / dApps** — keep on CV and in About chronology only
- **Generic "web development"** — web apps part of the offer but always paired with AI
- **"Full-stack developer"** as self-label — replace with "AI automation & product engineer" or "technical consultant"

### Secondary positioning (if AI automation feels wrong)

> *"Fractional technical partner for non-technical EU founders building MVPs on Next.js + AI."*

Lands you in fractional CTO territory (€150–350/hr in Germany, €140–300/hr in France, €160–320/hr in Netherlands; monthly retainers €3.5k–€6.5k for 10–25 hrs/week) but requires prior exit/scale-up credibility you may not yet have. Flagged as the `/partner` page on the site.

## Pricing: €60/hour is fine as a floor, wrong as the primary offer

Your current €60/hour is market-correct for a Slovak senior on a non-productised hourly model (Slovak senior freelance band is €55–95/hour per Index.dev 2025 data), but it caps your income at roughly €9k/month at full utilisation, and sends the wrong buying signal to AI-buying clients who expect outcome pricing.

**Keep €60/hr as your fallback contract rate for ad-hoc client work, but lead with three productised offers:**

### 1. AI Automation Roadmap — €2,500 (2 weeks)

2-week paid discovery engagement inspired by Brennan Dunn's Roadmapping model. You audit the client's workflows, deliver a written report (not a proposal) identifying 3–5 automations with estimated ROI. This is your **lead filter** and your **highest-conversion offer** to larger projects. About **60–70% convert into the next tier.**

### 2. AI Workflow Implementation — €6,000–12,000 (4–6 weeks)

Fixed-scope n8n + Next.js + LLM build (chatbot, RAG document Q&A, CRM automation, or internal tool). Benchmarked against Ciela AI data: setup fees $3.5k–$10k for most automation categories.

### 3. AI Operations Retainer — €1,500–2,500/month (min 3 months)

Monitoring, iteration, new workflow additions, support. Nick Saraev's LeftClick scales on $1.5k–$3.5k/month retainer blocks; this is **the model that produces predictable income.**

## Optional track — add targeted outreach later (months 3+)

Cold outreach is **not required** for this plan to work — the inbound stack above will produce clients on its own. However, if you want to accelerate pipeline once inbound is validated, here is the outreach spectrum ranked from lowest to highest friction:

### Level 1 — LinkedIn warm outreach (recommended, not "cold")

- 5 targeted DMs per week to founders / COOs whose posts you've genuinely engaged with
- Research each recipient 2–3 minutes before sending
- Reference a specific detail of their business or recent post
- Ask a question about their pain, not pitch your service
- Feels like professional networking, not spam
- **Expected**: 20–25% reply rate, 1 discovery call per 6–8 DMs

### Level 2 — Platform-routed targeted outreach

- Malt + Lemon.io + YunoJuno let you apply to briefs clients post
- You're pitching inside their platform with full context on what they're asking for
- Not cold in the traditional sense — they've signalled buying intent
- Platform fees cover the matching they do (Malt 10%, Lemon.io 0% direct, YunoJuno 0%)

### Level 3 — Optional cold email (only if you want it)

If you decide to add traditional cold email in month 3+ after inbound is humming:

- Buy 2 secondary domains (`pobocek-team.com`, `getpobocek.com`) — $20/year
- Set up mailboxes in Google Workspace (~$6/user/month)
- 3-week warmup in Instantly ($37/month)
- Targeting per GDPR:
  - **Legal-easy cold email markets:** UK (PECR exempts B2B), France (CNIL allows profession-related B2B), Netherlands, Slovakia/Czechia (LIA + opt-out), Sweden/Finland/Norway
  - **LinkedIn-only markets:** Germany (UWG §7 requires prior consent), Denmark (near-ban)
- Volume math: 30 emails/day per mailbox, 2 mailboxes × 2 domains = 120/day max, 150 replies/month → 10–20 qualified calls → 2–4 signed contracts at mature efficiency
- Personalisation is no longer optional: pure template sends get <2% reply; segment-of-one first emails get 5–10% consistently
- Send Monday launch, Wednesday/Thursday peak, 9:30–11:30 AM recipient local time; Friday is worst
- Follow-ups generate 42% of all replies — send 3 follow-ups minimum across 17 days

**Cost if activated:** Instantly Growth ($37/mo) + Clay starter ($149/mo, pause in low-outreach months) + LinkedIn Sales Navigator Core ($99/mo) = ~$185/month tooling.

**Only add this after months 1–3 of inbound has produced 2+ signed clients.** If inbound works, you may never need it.

### Cultural note on outreach for your market

Czech/Slovak B2B buyers prefer LinkedIn + warm referrals over US-style high-volume cold email. Personalised Slovak/Czech-language openers convert far better than English mass sends. Your local outreach volume should be 10–20% of total, all manual, all in Slovak.

## Content strategy on €0/month

The content goal in the first 90 days is not audience growth — it is **producing durable assets that show up in Google results and LinkedIn search when specific prospects investigate you after a warm intro or Malt profile view.** Post to skeptical lurkers, not followers.

### Three content surfaces, in order of leverage

1. **LinkedIn founder-led account, 3 posts/week.** Every post is a real number from a real build: *"I replaced a client's 4-hour daily review-moderation job with a 40-line n8n + GPT-4o workflow. Here's the exact flow."* This is the highest-ROI platform for B2B dev/AI consulting in 2025–26. Founder-mode accounts massively outperform company pages. Inboxpirates Consulting generated 18 meetings and 12 qualified leads (including Freshworks, Adobe, Gartner) from organic LinkedIn with minimal ad spend.
2. **One technical case study per month on pobocek.dev.** Full-length, 1,500–2,500 words, with code, screenshots, and the pricing you charged. Eviro is your first case study, written up as *"How I built an automated review-management system for local businesses on a bootstrapped budget — and what it costs to clone."* Each post is a durable SEO asset.
3. **A fortnightly newsletter (Beehiiv free tier up to 2,500 subscribers)** titled something like *"AI Automation Notes — EU edition"*. Content is a weekly n8n workflow breakdown + one client takeaway. Brennan Dunn built Double Your Freelancing into a 7-figure business from a weekly newsletter starting from consulting clients; this is the compounding asset.

**Skip YouTube for now** — production cost is too high versus text-first channels at your budget and stage.

### Named examples proving the inbound path

- **Danny Postma (NL)** — documented everything publicly on X + SEO; HeadshotPro hit $300k MRR. First meaningful SEO traffic at ~3 months, serious traffic at 6+ months. Pattern: audiences follow results, not vice versa.
- **Marc Lou (FR)** — 20k newsletter subscribers, 95k X followers over 3 years, 21 shipped products; $80–95k/month in early 2026. First meaningful revenue came ~18 months in from the newsletter, but ShipFast (his productised boilerplate) converted it into cashflow much faster.
- **Inboxpirates Consulting** — 192k organic impressions on one viral LinkedIn post + gated Google Docs lead magnets = 18 meetings, 12 qualified leads (Freshworks, Adobe, Gartner), 40.6% LinkedIn connection acceptance rate.

### Timeline expectations (realistic cold start)

- LinkedIn posting 3×/week: first conversations in 4–8 weeks, first paid client 3–6 months.
- pobocek.dev blog / SEO: 6–12 months to meaningful inbound. Do it anyway — it compounds.
- Newsletter: 3–6 months to a subscriber base (300–500) that reliably converts 1–2 clients.

## Eviro + pobocek.dev: leverage, then delay, then converge

The evidence from Pieter Levels, Jon Yongfook (Bannerbear), Marc Lou, Adam Wathan, Arvid Kahl, Brennan Dunn, and Justin Jackson all points to the same structural lesson: **the SaaS and the consultancy are synergistic only if you sequence them correctly, and they destroy each other if you run them as equal parallel priorities.**

### Do immediately

1. **Make Eviro the hero case study on pobocek.dev.** Title it *"How I built and ship an AI review-automation SaaS as a solo dev — architecture, costs, mistakes."* This single page does more for your credibility than any paid ad ever could. It proves you ship production AI systems for SMB customers — which is exactly what your consulting buyers need to believe. *(Status: case study published, with problem/solution/outcomes structure.)*
2. **Put "Founder of Eviro" in your LinkedIn headline** next to "AI Automation Consultant." Dual-role founders close consulting deals at materially higher rates; the SaaS signals commitment and proof of technical end-to-end capability.
3. **Use Eviro's email list and customer conversations as primary market research** for your consulting offers. Every Eviro support ticket is a potential €6k consulting build for a larger client with the same pain.

### Do not do (months 1–12)

- **Do not split development time 50/50 between Eviro and consulting** in the first year. Justin Jackson documented burnout/depression doing exactly this; Jon Yongfook explicitly refused to consult at all to protect Bannerbear's development pace. Your consulting pays the bills — **Eviro gets maintenance mode + one weekend day** for the first 6–12 months.
- **Do not advertise Eviro prominently as a competing service** ("hire me to build you an Eviro clone") while you're selling consulting. It dilutes the consulting positioning. Refer to Eviro as *"my SaaS,"* link to it once, and move on.

### Re-evaluate at month 12

- If Eviro hits ~€2–5k MRR organically → shift to Jon Yongfook's 50/50 rule and productise your remaining consulting into fixed-scope retainers.
- If Eviro passes ~€8–10k MRR → raise consulting rates aggressively or wind consulting down per the Jackson/Wathan model.
- If Eviro is still <€500 MRR at month 12 → demote it to pure case-study status and stop actively developing it.

### The pattern that works

Brennan Dunn's sequence:

```
consulting → productised service → info product → SaaS
```

This is the exact ladder for a solo operator with your stack:

- Consulting funds runway
- Productised consulting (your €2.5k roadmap + €6–12k implementations) removes time-for-money
- An info product or newsletter captures audience
- The SaaS (Eviro) captures the bottom of the funnel once there's enough audience to feed it

**Do not skip steps.**

## Expected outcomes and what to measure

### Realistic target by end of month 3 (inbound-only)

- **1–3 clients total** — approximately 0–1 from Malt, 1–2 from warm network, 0–1 from content/referrals
- **Monthly revenue: €3–6k** at this stage (not €10k). Anyone promising faster is selling a course.

- **3–5 clients total** (mix of recurring retainers + fixed-scope builds)
- **Monthly revenue: €5–10k**
- **Newsletter subscribers: 300–500**
- **LinkedIn followers: +300–500 from baseline**

### Core KPIs to track weekly

- LinkedIn post impressions (target 3k+ per post by month 3)
- Malt proposal-to-contract rate (target 20%+)
- Newsletter subscribers (target 200+ by month 3)
- Warm network reactivation replies
- **Signed contract value per week** — the only metric that actually matters, everything else is noise

### The three failure modes to watch for

1. **Fragmenting across too many platforms** — run 3 channels deeply, not 8 shallowly
2. **Returning to generalist positioning** when AI-automation leads are slow ("I also do web development" kills the positioning)
3. **Giving Eviro more time than it earns** — if it's not producing MRR, it's producing case studies, and case studies take one writeup, not five weekends
